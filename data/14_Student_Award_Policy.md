## **1. Purpose and Principles** 

This policy is to establish clear principles and a transparent framework for the establishment and management of student honors and awards at VinUniversity. All student recognition, honors and awards must be established and administered with full transparency and under the guidelines in this policy. 

This policy serves the following purposes: 

- to reflect on a holistic education approach and support the mission of developing talents for the future of VinUniversity. 

- to implement a transparent, clear, fair, practical process and procedures for recognizing students with honors and awards. 

## **2. Area of application** 

This policy applies to all full-time, current students of VinUniversity and recognizes achievements in both academic and non-academic matters. 

## **3. Content** 

## **3.1 Student Performance Evaluation** 

Students will be evaluated in a holistic approach that covers both academic and non-academic performance. The University’s formal evaluation will be implemented by the end of the academic year. The results will be used for: 

- Granting honors or awards to outstanding students for the academic year. 

- Reviewing all requests for other kinds of awards. 

By the end of the academic year, all students are supposed to complete **the Self-evaluation and Award Nomination forms** in the Student Success Platform. _(See Appendix 1 for further instruction)._

## **4. Documentation and Feature of Student Honors and Awards** 

- **4.1** Honors (President’s List and Dean’s List) based on academic merit or other academic achievements shall be listed on the transcript of academic record in accordance with the current regulations. 

- **4.2** Honors based on criteria that do not include academic merit, or other academic achievements shall not be listed on the transcript of academic record unless otherwise approved by the Provost. 

- **4.3** Hall of Inspiration is used to feature senior students who have been listed on the President’s List at least once during their studies. 

- **4.4** Graduation Cords are used to feature graduates who have been listed on President’s List and Changemaker Awards or EXCEL Awards at least once during their studies. (The EXCEL awards were applied to the academic years of 2020-2023). 

- **4.5** Champions of Change Wall is used to feature students who have been listed for President’s Award, Provost’s Award, Dean’s Award, Changemaker Award and Champions of Change Awards.

## **5. How to apply** 

## **5.1 For Honors** 

Students do not need to apply. Instead, they will be nominated by their host College based on academic excellence as measured by the GPA/CGPA and other EXCEL attributes achievements. Selected students are placed on the Dean’s List at the end of the regular semesters in recognition of their academic achievement in that semester. The Dean of the College will inform students in writing if they are placed on the Dean’s List. 

## **5.2 For Scholarships and Awards** 

Students can apply for Scholarships and/or Awards at the end of each academic year or when the application period opens. Students must apply via the Student Success Platform following the call for applications announced by the Student Affairs Management Office. The results will be announced at the beginning of the new academic year. 

## **6. Disbursement** 

The award and grant amounts are exclusively valid for full-time VinUniversity students. In cases where a student withdraws, the scholarship will no longer be valid. In cases where a student takes a temporary leave of absence, the scholarship and grant will only be valid for the semester during which the student returns to study at VinUniversity. 

The award and grant will be credited directly to the students' university accounts and will be deducted from their tuition and fees. Any excess or shortfall will be adjusted in subsequent semesters until fully utilized. In cases where students have no outstanding fees and have already received a full scholarship, the scholarship will be disbursed to them in cash. 

## **7. Complaints and Appeals** 

Students have the right to appeal or to give feedback regarding the University’s decisions concerning specific awards, within 10 working days after the decision is announced. Based on an official appeal, request or feedback, the Provost will review the case and revise the decision if needed. 

Students who believe at any time that a conflict of interest has arisen concerning an award must bring this to the attention of the Student Affairs Management and Registrar Office.

## **9. Definitions** 

- **9.1** _**Honor**_ is bestowed as a recognition of students’ outstanding achievements and refers to a formal title. Examples of honors include the President’s List and Dean’s List. These distinctions typically reflect high academic performance or exceptional contributions in specific areas. 

- **9.2** _**Award**_ is a broad term encompassing any type of recognition or financial support given to a student for their achievements, contributions, or specific characteristics. Awards can include scholarships, grants, prizes, and other forms of acknowledgment. 

- **9.3** _**Scholarship**_ is a financial award given to a student based on academic achievement, merit, or other specific criteria _**.**_ 

- **9.4** _**Grant**_ is a financial award given to a student based on financial need, academic achievement, or specific criteria set by the grantor. 

- **9.5** _**Eligibility criteria**_ are the standards and benchmarks that all candidates must fulfill to be considered for an award. The eligibility criteria must be defined in the conditions of the award and must be fair, meet the intent of the achievement being recognized, yet broad and inclusive enough to result in sufficient numbers of people being eligible and to attract sufficient applicants. 

6 

- **9.6** _**Selection criteria**_ are the standards and benchmarks that the selection committee/panel uses to rank applicants and select award recipients. The selection criteria must be defined in the conditions of the award and are the only criteria to be used in the process of ranking and selecting potential recipients. 

- **9.7** _**Donor**_ is any person, entity, or organization donating or other benefit to VinUniversity for the establishment or maintenance of an award, with no expectation of a return of material or intellectual or any other benefit, except name recognition as approved by the Award Committee. 

- **9.8** _**Sponsor**_ refers to a person, organization, or government that provides funds and support in the manner of sponsorship to the University or its students. Sponsors are different from donors in that they receive specified material benefits in return for their support. For example, an entire could sponsor a project or an initiative or activity and announce that individuals who deliver a certain quality of final product will receive an award. 

- **9.9** _**Benefit**_ refers to the payment or other advantages that an award recipient is given or receives as part of their scholarship or prize (for example a direct monetary award, a tuition fee relief or exemption, receipt of goods and services, an internship, recognition for meritorious achievements or community service, etc.). 

- **9.10** _**Recipient**_ is a beneficiary of a scholarship or prize, has been accepted and, awards are a written receipt of an award.
